# Brazil Air Traffic Data 2000–2025 (ANAC)

The National Civil Aviation Agency (ANAC) is the regulatory authority responsible for overseeing, regulating, and developing the civil aviation sector in Brazil. Established in 2005, ANAC operates under the Ministry of Ports and Airports with the mission of ensuring safety, efficiency, transparency, and competitiveness in civil aviation activities. Its responsibilities include the supervision of aircraft, airports, airlines, and related services, as well as the production and dissemination of official statistical data and open datasets related to the Brazilian air transport market.

Within the scope of air transport statistical data, ANAC provides several structured databases, including both aggregated datasets and detailed microdata that enable in-depth analyses of air transport demand and supply in Brazil. One of the main statistical products is the Demand and Supply Report, which compiles monthly data series since 2000, covering sector performance indicators such as flights, passengers, cargo, aircraft operations, and routes. These datasets support market intelligence, economic research, regulatory monitoring, and public policy evaluation.

The microdata bases consist of disaggregated operational records, in which each row represents detailed information about a specific flight stage. These datasets allow granular analysis of routes, operational performance, airline participation, and temporal patterns. According to the official variable documentation, the microdata follow standardized regulatory guidelines and maintain consistency with ANAC’s broader statistical reporting framework.

Variables presented here are related to those described in Ordinances No. 3,506 and 3,507, of the Superintendence of Air Services of the National Civil Aviation Agency, both of November 11, 2019.

The Microdata of Statistical Data are in the same standard, and at the same level of aggregation, that are in the entire database of the Agency. Therefore, they are distributed in their two dimensions, that is, in basic steps and combined steps, as described below.

Basic Stage (flight stage): The basic steps are those performed by the aircraft from its takeoff to the next landing, regardless of where the embarkation or disembarkation of the transport object took place. The statistical data of the basic stages represent the status of the aircraft at each stage of the flight, presenting the movement of cargo and passengers between the aerodromes of origin and destination of the aircraft. It is the operation of an aircraft between a takeoff and the next landing, the connection between two airfields.

Combined Stage (On flight origin and destination - OFOD): The combined steps identify the pairs of origin, where the boarding of the transport object occurred, and destination, where the landing of the transport object occurred, regardless of the existence of intermediate aerodromes, served by a given flight. It is the flight stage seen with a focus on the transport object (people and/or loads), based on boarding and disembarkation at related airfields. The statistical data of the combined stage inform the origin and destination of the passengers and cargo transported on the flight, regardless of the stopovers made.

These datasets are particularly valuable for researchers, data scientists, aviation economists, policymakers, and industry analysts seeking to understand traffic trends, operational efficiency, passenger demand behavior, market structure, and the historical evolution of the Brazilian air transport sector. The microdata complement other open datasets available through ANAC’s Open Data Portal, such as airfare commercialization data, aircraft movement statistics, and consolidated performance indicators for the aviation industry.
